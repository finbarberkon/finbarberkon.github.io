Lenovo S10e Revival

What

Ultimately, I want to install a lightweight command line only Linux distro on my S10e and use it to get better at living in the command line and using Linux generally, and maybe running a simple server from it.

Why

The Lenovo S10e, for anybody who was not a year 9 student in an Australian public school in 2009, was part of a short-lived government program to provide all public school students with a computer which they could use both at home and at school.

I stumbled accross this relic a few months ago among some old stuff from high school and remembered how much I loved the thing. It is virtualy indestructible, has a replaceable battery (!!), a smorgasbord of ports, manages to fit a surprisingly ergonomic keyboard and into a very small form factor, and has a bold metallic-flake maroon finish. All the trappings of a pro machine, I say!

Unfortunately, the laptops have one major flaw: they have a BIOS supervisor password and have a heavily controlled version of Windows 8 installed. No chance of getting admin privileges to modify anything, and no way to modify the boot order to run an installer for a different OS.

With a 1.60 GHz Atom CPU and a measly 1GB of RAM, it hardly seems worth the hassle. And it probably isn't. But, for years I wished I could get around the restrictions on this thing, and maybe I finally can...

How

After a lot of googling it seems I have two options:

1. I try and reflash the BIOS to wipe the supervisor password.

2. I rely on good old fashioned hardware security flaws, and take the hard drive out, install the OS I want on it, and put it back in.
